The ED (Education) Visa is for foreigners enrolled in full-time language courses, degree programs, or other approved educational courses in Thailand. It is one of the most popular stepping-stone visas for long-term stay.

Nationality and Eligibility Overview

Nationality and Eligibility Overview

Open to citizens of all countries, including the United States. The main requirement is enrollment in a full-time course at a recognized Thai educational institution (language school, university, vocational school, etc.).

Detailed Requirements

Detailed Requirements

Key requirements include:

  • Enrollment: Full-time enrollment in an approved course (minimum 15-20 hours per week for language courses).
  • Institution: The school or university must be recognized by the Thai Ministry of Education.
  • Financial proof: Proof of ability to support yourself during studies (bank statements or sponsor letter).
  • Attendance: Must maintain good attendance (usually minimum 80% for extensions).
Application Process and Documents

Application Process and Documents

You can apply at a Thai Embassy/Consulate abroad with an acceptance letter from the school, or change status from another visa (such as Tourist) at an Immigration Bureau office inside Thailand. Required documents include passport, acceptance letter from school, application form, financial proof, photos, and sometimes a medical certificate. The initial visa is usually granted for 1 year (or the duration of the course) and can be extended annually as long as you remain enrolled and meet attendance requirements.

Current Rules and Pending Changes (as of July 16, 2026)

Current Rules and Pending Changes (as of July 16, 2026)

As of July 16, 2026, the ED visa remains one of the most popular and accessible long-stay options. Language schools (especially Thai language courses) are commonly used as a stepping stone to other visas. Attendance and enrollment requirements are strictly enforced.

Pros and Cons

Pros and Cons

Pros

  • Relatively easy to obtain if enrolled in a recognized school.
  • Can be extended annually as long as you remain a student.
  • Common stepping stone to other long-stay visas (Non-O, DTV, Marriage, etc.).
  • Allows part-time work in some cases with permission.

Cons

  • Must attend classes regularly (minimum attendance required for extensions).
  • Cannot work full-time without permission.
  • Must pay tuition fees and remain enrolled.
  • Not suitable if you do not want to study.
Requirements Checklist

Requirements Checklist

  • Acceptance letter from recognized Thai school/university
  • Proof of financial support
  • Valid passport
  • Application form and photos
  • Medical certificate (sometimes required)
Practical Tips, Common Pitfalls, and Related Considerations

Practical Tips, Common Pitfalls, and Related Considerations

The ED visa is one of the most popular stepping-stone visas in Thailand. Many foreigners start with a Thai language course at a recognized school in Bangkok, Pattaya, or Chiang Mai, then transition to other visas (Non-O, DTV, Marriage, etc.).

Choose a reputable school that is properly registered with the Ministry of Education. Poor-quality or fake schools can cause problems with visa extensions.

You must maintain good attendance (usually 80% or higher) to get extensions. Missing too many classes is a common reason for visa problems.

Sources and Verification

Sources and Verification

  • Ministry of Education (recognized schools list)
  • Thai Embassy websites
