Thailand Tourist Visa (TR): Single Entry 60-Day Visa

The Thailand Tourist Visa (TR) is a single-entry visa that allows eligible foreign nationals to stay in Thailand for up to 60 days.

The Thailand Tourist Visa (TR) is a single-entry visa that allows eligible foreign nationals to stay in Thailand for up to 60 days. It is suitable for travelers who do not qualify for visa exemption or who prefer to apply for a visa in advance for greater certainty and flexibility.

Nationality and Eligibility Overview

Nationality and Eligibility Overview

The Tourist Visa (TR) is available to most nationalities, including those who qualify for visa exemption. It is particularly useful for passport holders from countries not on the visa exemption list or for travelers who want a formal visa stamp before arrival. United States citizens can apply for the TR visa if they prefer the security of a pre-approved visa or need to show proof of legal entry for other purposes.

Detailed Requirements

Detailed Requirements

Applicants must meet the following requirements:

  • Valid passport: Passport must have at least 6 months validity remaining from the date of entry into Thailand.
  • Visa application form: Completed and signed application form (available from Thai embassies or the e-Visa portal).
  • Recent photograph: One or two passport-sized photos (usually 4x6 cm or as specified by the embassy).
  • Proof of travel: Confirmed flight itinerary showing entry and onward/return travel within 60 days.
  • Proof of accommodation: Hotel bookings or invitation letter from a host in Thailand.
  • Proof of sufficient funds: Bank statements, traveler’s cheques, or credit card statements showing at least 20,000 THB (or equivalent) per person.
  • Travel insurance (recommended): Some embassies may request proof of travel insurance covering Thailand.
Application Process and Documents

Application Process and Documents

Where to Apply

You can apply at a Royal Thai Embassy or Consulate in your country of residence, or through Thailand’s official e-Visa portal (if available for your nationality). Processing times vary but are typically 3–7 business days for e-Visa and longer for paper applications at embassies.

Visa Fee

The fee for a single-entry Tourist Visa is usually around 1,000 THB (approximately USD 30), though it can vary slightly depending on the embassy and nationality. Payment is normally made in local currency or as specified by the embassy.

Extending Your Stay

Once in Thailand, you can extend the Tourist Visa for an additional 30 days at any Immigration Bureau office. The extension fee is 1,900 THB. The extension is granted at the discretion of the immigration officer.

Current Rules and Pending Changes (as of July 16, 2026)

Current Rules and Pending Changes (as of July 16, 2026)

As of July 16, 2026, the standard Tourist Visa (TR) remains a single-entry visa valid for 60 days of stay, extendable once for 30 days. Thailand continues to promote its e-Visa system for faster processing. No major changes to the core TR visa rules have been announced recently, though processing and requirements can vary by embassy.

Pros and Cons

Pros and Cons

Pros

  • Provides a formal visa stamp before travel, which can be useful for certain nationalities or travel purposes.
  • 60-day initial stay (longer than the older 30-day visa exemption in some cases).
  • Can be extended once for 30 days inside Thailand.
  • Available to almost all nationalities.

Cons

  • Requires advance application and supporting documents.
  • Processing time and fees vary by embassy.
  • Single entry only (you must leave and re-apply for another TR if you exit Thailand).
  • More paperwork than using visa exemption for eligible nationalities.
Requirements Checklist

Requirements Checklist

  • Valid passport with at least 6 months validity
  • Completed visa application form
  • Recent passport-sized photograph(s)
  • Confirmed flight itinerary (entry and onward/return)
  • Proof of accommodation in Thailand
  • Proof of sufficient funds (minimum ~20,000 THB per person)
  • Visa fee (approx. 1,000 THB)
  • Travel insurance (recommended or sometimes required)
Practical Tips, Common Pitfalls, and Related Considerations

Practical Tips, Common Pitfalls, and Related Considerations

The Tourist Visa (TR) is a good option if you want certainty before travel or if your nationality does not qualify for visa exemption. It is also useful if you need to show a formal visa for visa runs to neighboring countries or for other administrative purposes.

Common pitfalls include submitting incomplete documents, having insufficient proof of funds, or not having a clear onward travel plan. Always check the specific requirements of the Thai Embassy where you are applying, as they can vary slightly.

If you plan to stay longer than 90 days total, consider transitioning to other visa types such as ED (Education), DTV, or Non-O once in Thailand, rather than repeatedly applying for new Tourist Visas.
